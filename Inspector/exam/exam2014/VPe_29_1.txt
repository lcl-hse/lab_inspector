The graph gives us the data about the percentage of adult people (from 65 and older) in Japan, Sweden and USA from 1940 to 2040. 
USA demonstrates the most stable tendency of growing of population. Beginning with 10 percent in 1940, USA has grown from 1980's 15 percent to the 2030's 17- 18 percent. 
Japan has the lowest tendency throughout its history from 1940 to 2030 - it measures only from 3 to 10 percent, but 2030's become a new point in the development of population. The line of population grows up fast in 2030 to 25 percent and keeps a tendency to soar. 
Sweden line describes a tendency of growing of population every 20 years. The strongest chance of population was between 1980 and 2010 when there was difference in 7 percent (from 13 to 20). Sweden has the middle place (25 percent) between Japan (27 percent) and USA (23 percent)
