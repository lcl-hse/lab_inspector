The bar chart compares the amount of children, who do not have an opportunity to achieve primary education, in three regions and gender over the period of 12 years. It is clear that the total figures fro children without primary education decreased in all regions between 2000 and 2012. Also mostly in all regions the number of uneducated girls was more rather the same figures for boys, while in 2012 - less. In 2000 the number of children without access to primary education in Africa was 43,7 millions. And from this figure 23,7 millions of children are girls and 20 million boys. In 2010 the figures for both girls and boys slightly fell, while the percentage of boys and girls in total amount of the category remained the same. In the rest of the world the number of children who do not have opportunity to get education also fell between 2000 and 2012. The sharpest decrease can be noticed in South Asia especially when the figures are considered. The decrease in the number of girls was more steady. This resulted in the higher amount of girls without access to primary education rather than boys in the rest of the world.
