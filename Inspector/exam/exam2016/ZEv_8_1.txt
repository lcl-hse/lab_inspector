The bar chart demonstrates the discrepancy in the quantity of children without access to primary education by gender and region in a period of 12 years, from 2000 to 2012. Three regions are presented: Africa, South Asia and Rest of the World. 
In the demonstrated 12-year period, the highest number of kids without primary education is in Africa. There are approximately 33 millions of children without primary education in 2012 in Africa. In fact, the same number was in South Asia, but in 2000. Moreover, the quantity of children without primary education is South Asia reduced to 10 millions over the past 12 years, what is less than in the Rest of World in almost 1,5 times. 
Talking about the gender, there are more females than males without primary education in all regions in 2000. However, the situation changed in South Asia and Rest of World in 2012. 
To summarize, a huge downgrade is shown in the quantity of kids without access to primary education in a 12-year period.
