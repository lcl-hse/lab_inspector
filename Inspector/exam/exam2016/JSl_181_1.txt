The first graph reveals how the use of Facebook altered in 2013 from the results shown in 2012, whereas the second graph provides information about the results of a survey researching why men and women use Facebook. It is clearly shown that the use of the largest social network in the world in mobile devices increased almost twofold between March 2012 and March 2013. About 100 millions of people logged in Facebook using their handhelds during the period in question, while for desktop devices the respective figures slightly decreased from approximately 150 millions of users to less than 140 million. The overall number of users increased, though. As for why people use Facebook, the main reason for that is sharing photos and videos. Other incentives are receiving updates, discovering funny content and charity. In fact, women seen to be equally or more interested in all four ways of using listed than men. To summarizing, the overall use of Facebook greatly increased between March 2012 and March 2013 because of the users of mobile devices. The social network is used mostly for sharing content with less interested attracted to its other features.
