The first chart illustrate common tendencies in using Facebook in one-year period from March 2012 to March 2013. The second chart shows what people think are their main reasons for using Facebook. Second chart provides data for men and women separately. 
As it can be seen from the first chart the vast majority (150 out of 200 millions of people) prefered to use desktop version of Facebook instead of using its mobile app. Although to the March of 2013 users' preferences have not reaaly changed, the ratio of those using mobile version increased from being just over 50 millions in 2012 to almost 100 millions in 2013. 
The second chart given shows that nearly a half of women consider sharing photos and videos via Facebook among the main reasons for its using, whereas men see this reason equally important to the ability of recieving updates. Remarkable, that this function of Facebook is estimated to be relevant for the equal number of women (39%). 
To sum up, the ratio of people using Facebook app almost doubled in the period from 2012 to 2013. Reasons for using Facebook for men and women generally differ, however, the ability of recieving updates seems to be equally relevant for both men and women.
