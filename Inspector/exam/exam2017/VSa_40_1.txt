This pie chart represents data about two different ways of goods delivery. As it evidence from the graph, the great majority of metails transported by rail and food products by road. 
There are a lot of difference in the structure of transportation of goods in Eastern Europe in 2008. For example, only 2% of machinery delivered by road and approximately 12% by rail. Another difference in the structure was a percentage of food products delivery. 30% of food products transported by road and only 11% by rail. Moreover, only 11% of metails transported by road and as for rail, it was more approximately in three times (35%). By the mean, there was a common feature in the structure of transportation goods. For example, the percentage of chemicals was similar (11% and 16% by rail and road).
