The diagram illustrates how the unemployment rate changed from 2014 to 2015 in all the world and in certain regions such as North Africa, Middle East, European Union, Latin America and South Asia. 
As it can be seen from the graph, the percentage of unemployed people did not change for these two years in North Africa and the indicator remained to be 12,5 per cent. The number of people who could not find a job decreased in Middle East and unemployment rate fell down from 11 per cent to 9,6 per cent. There was a slight increase in the percentage of unemployed people from 7,5 per cent to 7,9 per cent in European Union. The level of unemployment declined from 6,8 per cent to 5,8 per cent in Latin America while in South Asia unemployment rate was stable at 3,9 per cent. The worldwide indicator did not change and made up 5,9 per cent during two years North Africa continued to have the highest unemployment rate among selected world regions in 2014 and 2015 whereas South Asia had the lowest one. The changes of indicator in Middle East, European Union and Latin America did not affect the worldwide unemployment from 2014 to 2015.
