The charts below provide information about goods transported by rail and road between countries in Eastern Europe in 2008. 
The percent of manufactured goods transported by rail and road was approximately similar (25% and 28%). By contrast, the percentage of metals transported by rail was 35%, while the percentage of metals transported by road was only 11%. 
A more detailed look at the charts reveals that chemicals and food products transported by rail had qual percentage (11%). Likewise, non-metallic minerals and metals transported by road had also approximately equal percentage. Besides, transportation of machinery by road had the lowest persentage (2%), while transportation of machinery by rail was 12% and had approximately equal percentage with food products (11%) and chemicals (11%). 
All in all, metals transported by rail had the biggest percentage, while the most popular good transported by road was food products.
