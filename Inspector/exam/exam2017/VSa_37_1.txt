The diagrams illustrate the persentage of goods, transported by rail and road between countries in Eastern Europe in 2008.

The biggest part of railway transportation is metals with 35%, while food is the most transporded good by road with 30%. For both ways of transportation almost third part is manufactured goods with 25% by rail and 28% by road. Chemicals and non-metallic minerals are more transported by road. This difference is not that significant as these two goods togeather have almost same parts of pie charts with 11% and about 3% by rail and 16% and 13% by road.
