The given pie charts provide the information about the amount of goods transported between countries in Eastern Europe in 2008. The first chart depicts goods transported by rail, and the second depicts goods transported by road in per cent. 
In general, the main feature of the first one is that the percentage of transported metals is the biggest, compared to the other goods (it is 35%). Manufactured goods have 25%, it is the second biggest part of all the transported goods. We can notice, that chemicals and food products share the same percentage - it is 11%. Obviously, the smallest part of the chart is non-metallic minerals - less then 10% of them were transported by rail in 2008. 
The second chart shows that unlike the first one, a lot of food products were transported by road (30%). Also manufactured goods has 28%, it is almost the same with the first chart. The percentage of transported chemicals is a little bigger than in the first one (16%). The smallest percentage if road transported goods have the machinery goods - it is only 2%.
