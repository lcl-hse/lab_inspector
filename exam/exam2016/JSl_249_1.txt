It can be seen in the graph that there were some changes in the number of children without access to primary school education from 2000 to 2012. The indicated changes differ boy gender and region. All in all, the graph shows a downward trend in the number of children without access to primary school education. The number of such boys in Africa fell from 20 millions in 2010 to 14,5 millions in 2012 at the same time the number of uneducated girls in Africa fell from 23,7 in 2000 to 18.2 in 2012. The graph depicts a sharp decrease in the number of girls without access to primary school from 21.6 millions in 2000 to 4.8 millions in 2012. Furthermore there was a gradual fall in the number of uneducated boys in South Asia from 11.1 in 2000 to 5.1 in 2012. In the rest of the world the number of children without access to primary school education decreased from 2000 to 2012 - more than 5 millions of boys and about 3 millions of girls.
