The given graph illustrates the variations in the percentage share of international smartphone profits for a five-year period of time, starting from 2010. Overall, from 2010 to 2015 not one company has had more than a half of the share profits, but Apple has shows a dramatical increase, gaining more than 90% in 2015. To begin, HTC had around 10%, in the beginning; although it went through marginal rise in 2011, now it has only 5% of the share. Wherease Blackberry started with just above 20%, the percentage has declined throughout the given period, finishing the last with around 4%. Samsung together with Apple increased its shares until 2012, were Apple's profits dipped, but recovered later. As opposed to Apple Samsung continued to rise until it reached its peak in 2013 at about 40%, but then felt a significant decline with around 16%, like in the beginning. Apple had two time bigger percentage that Blackberry in 2011, with 20 and 40% respectively. In 2015 Apple has almost all the share on the market with more than 90%.
