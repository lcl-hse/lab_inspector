The diagram illustrates the proportion of the population aged 65 and over from 1940 to 2040 in Japan, Sweden and in the USA. 
To begin with, the percentage of population in the USA aged 65 and over rose gradually from approximately 9% in 1940 to 15% in 1980. Then it is erratic until 2025. This percentage is going to begin to rocket since 2025. 
Secondly, the share of population aged 65 and over in Sweden increase dramatically. It reached approximately 14% in 1980. There was a slight decrease between 1980 and 1995. However, this percentage started to rocket in 1995 and was 20% in 2010. Then, it is decreasing until 2025. During last period this share is going to continue rocketing. 
Moreover, the percentage of population aged 65 and over in Japan fluctuated during the first period (from 1940 to 2005). It went up gradually since 2005. Furthermore, this share is going to rocket until 2040. 
Finally, the overall trend in three different countries is upward.
