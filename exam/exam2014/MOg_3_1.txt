The diagram below provides information about length, date of opening and amount of passengers per year on the underground railway systems in six cities. 
The oldest underground railway system is in London. It was opened in 1863. Also it is the longest railway system, its length is about 400 kilometres. However, there is a Tokyo railway system which has the biggest amount of passengers per year - it is near 2 billions. The underground railway system with the smallest amount of passengers per year is also situated in Japan - it was built in 1981 in Kyoto, it has only 45 million passengers per year. Furthermore, it is the shortest railway system with its 11 kilometres of route. The youngest underground railway system, which was opened in 2001 in L. A., has a similar amount of passengers per year but is more than twice longer than in Kyoto.
