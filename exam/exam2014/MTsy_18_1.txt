The pie charts show structures of population in Yemen and Italy by the age in 2000 and expectations for 2050. 
The half of Yemen population in 2000 are children under age 14. The next age group of people aged 15-59 years performs 46,3 per cent of Yemen's population. The smallest part of people of Yemen consists of people aged 60 year and more. According to the projections numbers of people in the age of 15-59 years will show a considerable increase in 2050 by 11 per cent, such as people aged 60 and more years by 2,1 percent. Percentage of children will become smaller. 
On the charts about population of Italy can be seen that percentage of people aged 15-59 years is biggest and it is 61,6 per cent. Moreover people in the age of 60 and more years consist of one quarter of population of Italy. Percentage of children under 14 is smallest and takes 14,3 percent. Through the expectations there will be growth in percentage op people in the age of 15-59 years. But numbers of people aged 60 and more years will jump to 42,3 percent. Percentage of children under 14 will not show so sharp increase, and will become smaller by 2,8 per cent than in 2000.
